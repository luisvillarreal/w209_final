Yield Curve as a Barometer for the Economy

Jayanth Srinivasa - Luis Villarreal

December 13th, 2018 - Data Visualization W209.3


The Yield Curve has become a popular topic entering the second half of 2018, as the Fed has been more proactive in raising short-term rates. Starting this month, the flattening of the Yield Curve has produced its first inversions for the first time in a decade. The purpose of this project is to provide the user rich-data insights through visualizations and let her understand why the state of the Yield Curve has many economists spinning their heads. While we recognize there is no accurate way to predict large swings in the Markets, we believe this would provide a better idea of what to expect when the Curve becomes flat or inverted, and weather the storm. For the last six decades, whenever the Curve is inverted, a few months later the Markets have experienced high volatility and recessions in most times. We invite the user, first, to go through different dates and observe how the Curve changes in time. Second, change to the tab "SP500 vs YC" on the top left corner of the chart and hover along the datapoints in the S&P 500 and see how the Yield Curve behaves, especially before the big crashes in 2001 and 2008.




The following visualization allows the user to compare the spreads of the yield curve (top chart) and see the effects on the different indicators : GDP, S&P500 and Unemployment in the (bottom chart) a few months later. We invite the viewer to spot whenever the spreads go negative and see how many months it takes to reflect on the GDP (and other indicators) by adjusting the width of the red handlebars. The visualization also captures the points within the red handlebar and presents two scatterplot, one representing the daily S&P500 change vs the yield spread and the other with the GDP vs yield spread. In case the user would like to use the unemployment, S&P500 data instead of the GDP data, the user can do so by choosing the appropriate radio button.
